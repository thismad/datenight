Candidate 01 · Italy

Ischia

the volcanic spa island

Capri's cooler, less crowded sister in the Bay of Naples. A volcanic island where hot springs bubble up between beaches, lemon groves and pastel fishing villages. We land in Naples separately, then take the hydrofoil across together — our first joint mission.

What we'd do

  • Soak in the thermal gardens — dozens of hot pools terraced above the sea
  • Rent a scooter, circle the island, stop wherever it looks good
  • Dinner in Sant'Angelo, a car-free village that runs on seafood and slow evenings
  • Sunset at Castello Aragonese — a castle on its own rock in the sea

Why it works

  • Thermal spas — a built-in plan for every hour of the day
  • Real Italy — more locals than influencers
  • Warm sea + volcanic sand beaches
  • The food. Oh, the food.

Full disclosure

  • The journey — flight, transfer, then a 50-min hydrofoil
  • Hot — 30–33 °C, though the sea fixes that
  • It's an island — if I'm less charming than my voice notes, you're stuck with me until the ferry

Candidate 02 · France

Castles & a Convertible

the road trip

You fly to Paris. I'm at arrivals with a convertible. We drive south into the Loire Valley and spend three nights sleeping in actual castles — the kind with four-poster beds, creaky staircases and their own vineyards.

What we'd do

  • Top down through the valley — you own the playlist (enormous trust exercise)
  • Chambord & Chenonceau, the two most ridiculous castles France ever built
  • Nights in château hotels — sleep like 17th-century nobility
  • Riverside picnic: market cheese, baguette, a wine we pretend to understand

Why it works

  • Mildest weather of the four — 25–30 °C
  • Zero ferries, zero transfers — the drive is the date
  • Château hotels are weirdly affordable
  • Never a dull moment — castles just keep appearing

Full disclosure

  • No sea — best case, a château pool
  • I'll be driving — hands at ten and two (tragic)
  • Paris traffic in July is its own boss level

Candidate 03 · Greece

Corfu

the green Greek island

The greenest island in Greece — a Venetian old town, olive groves running down to the water, and coves so turquoise they look edited. They are not edited. This is the “we live at the beach and make zero difficult decisions” option.

What we'd do

  • Rent our own little boat (no licence needed) and hop along the west-coast coves
  • Swim at Paleokastritsa and Porto Timoni — see photo, believe photo
  • Evening wander in Corfu Town's Venetian alleys, dinner in a backstreet taverna
  • Absolutely nothing, scheduled daily at 2 pm sharp

Why it works

  • Best swimming of the four — bathwater-warm sea
  • Direct summer flights for both of us
  • Taverna dinners: long, loud, generous
  • A self-drive boat — best first-date vehicle ever invented

Full disclosure

  • The hottest option — 30–34 °C and a bit humid
  • July crowds in the old town
  • Pebbly beaches in places — I'll carry the water shoes; romance is logistics

Candidate 04 · Italy

Lake Como

the dolce vita

A lake in the Alps lined with 19th-century villas, cypress gardens and villages that look painted on. We take boats everywhere, eat gelato at an irresponsible rate, and pretend we're old money. George Clooney lives here — that's between him and us.

What we'd do

  • Rent a boat and swim in the middle of the lake, mountains on every side
  • Ferry-hop between Bellagio and Varenna — pastel villages, zero cars needed
  • Villa del Balbianello — Star Wars and Bond were filmed there; it's even better in person
  • Aperitivo at golden hour, lakeside, every single evening. Non-negotiable.

Why it works

  • Most romantic scenery per square metre in Europe
  • Cooler than the islands — alpine evenings
  • Effortless — boats and ferries do all the work
  • If conversation pauses, the view finishes the sentence

Full disclosure

  • Priciest of the four — the villas set the tone and the prices
  • Not a beach place — you swim off jetties and boats
  • Milan + ~1h transfer to reach the lake
